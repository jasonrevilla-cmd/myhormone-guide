For men with low testosterone, the two methods most consistently cited for effectiveness are pellets and injections — and EvexiPEL is one of the most widely used pellet systems specifically optimized for male dosing. Here's how these two options compare on the factors that matter most to men choosing between them.

## The Core Difference: Steady Levels vs. Clinical Flexibility

EvexiPEL pellets maintain stable testosterone levels continuously for 5–6 months per cycle. Injections produce predictable peaks and troughs that some men find disruptive — but which give providers far more control over dosing adjustments.

Neither is categorically superior. The right choice depends on your lifestyle, how you respond to peaks and troughs, and how much you value dose flexibility vs. management-free consistency.

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Factor | EvexiPEL Pellets | Testosterone Injections |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Cycle duration** | 5–6 months per insertion | Weekly or biweekly indefinitely |
| **Level consistency** | Very consistent; minimal fluctuation | Peaks 24–48h post-injection; trough before next dose |
| **Dose adjustment** | Not adjustable mid-cycle | Adjustable every injection |
| **Administration** | In-office procedure; ~15 min | Self-inject at home or clinic visit |

| **Hematocrit management** | Requires monitoring; donation if elevated | Same requirement |

## EvexiPEL for Men

EvexiPEL uses the Turek-based dosing methodology adapted for men, typically factoring in weight, symptom severity, and baseline lab values. Testosterone pellets for men are larger than those for women and dissolve over a longer cycle — most men return every 5–6 months.

**Why men choose it:** Stable testosterone levels across the entire cycle means stable energy, libido, mood, and body composition support — without weekly management. Many men who've been on injections describe the transition to pellets as eliminating the "rollercoaster" they didn't realize was affecting them.

**The EvexiPEL-specific advantage:** EvexiPEL's mentorship model means newer providers have access to experienced practitioners for case guidance — which can matter significantly for male dosing, where optimization requires more judgment than female protocols.

**What to know going in:** Because male testosterone doses are substantially higher than female doses, over-dosing is a more consequential problem. Elevated hematocrit (thickened blood) is the most common side effect requiring management. Experienced EvexiPEL providers dose conservatively on first insertions and re-test at 4–6 weeks.

## Testosterone Injections

Testosterone cypionate and enanthate are the most prescribed forms of TRT in the US. Weekly injections of 100–200mg are the standard starting protocol; some providers use twice-weekly lower doses to reduce peak-trough amplitude.

**Why men choose them:** Lower cost. Greater dose flexibility — if labs or symptoms indicate a needed change, the provider adjusts the next injection. This matters more than most men realize in the first 6–12 months of TRT, when optimal dosing is still being established.

**What the research shows:** The comparative outcomes data between pellets and injections is limited. A 2019 study in the *Journal of Sexual Medicine* found comparable improvements in sexual function and quality of life between pellet and injection methods. Level consistency differences between methods haven't been definitively linked to different clinical outcomes in controlled trials — though the patient experience data strongly favors pellets for subjective symptom control.

## A Practical Protocol Many Providers Use

Many TRT-experienced physicians start men on injections for 3–6 months to establish optimal dosing, then transition to pellets once the right dose is known. This avoids the primary pellet risk (over-dosing an inexperienced patient) while eventually capturing the consistency advantage that most men prefer for long-term management.

If your provider offers this kind of sequenced approach, it's a good sign they're thinking about optimization rather than just starting a protocol.

## How to Choose

**Choose EvexiPEL (or pellets) if:** You've already had some TRT experience and know your approximate dose, you value consistency over flexibility, and you want to minimize the weekly management burden.

**Choose injections if:** You're new to TRT and want to establish optimal dosing with more adjustability, cost is a primary concern, or you prefer not to undergo a procedure.
